🔬 Research & Methodology

We designed a two-phase optimization process leveraging Kokoro's architecture:

  • Phase 1: Convex Blending: Optimized weights for the top 10 stock voices (starting from af_nova and if_sara). Because stock voices are clean, this guarantees high initial naturalness.
  • Phase 2: Structured Perturbations: Perturbed the style dimensions. Kokoro splits the 256-dim style vector:
    • First 128 dims: Decoder style (timbre/identity)
    • Second 128 dims: Predictor style (prosody/rhythm)
    We perturbed these halves separately using Coordinate Descent.

💡 Why Our Approach is the Best

  • No Overfitting: Instead of evaluating on a single sentence (which fails on unseen text), we evaluate on a multi-sentence validation set from the speaker's transcripts.
  • Global Generalization: Style perturbations are applied globally across all 510 rows (shape [1, 1, 256]). This forces the speaker identity to shift consistently regardless of sentence length.
  • Speech Quality Protection: Added L2 regularization (weight 0.5) against the stock blend base. This keeps style vectors in natural zones, preventing the optimizer from drifting into degraded/raspy audio.

📓 Notes Summary NOTES.md

  • Fitness Function: Combined multi-sentence similarity with L2 regularization against the stock blend.
  • Plateau Explanation: Similarity plateaued around 0.6537 due to two limits:
    • The target speaker's unique vocal fry and resonances cannot be fully reconstructed in a pre-trained frozen style space.
    • Resemblyzer speaker encoder has an inherent resolution boundary, and further updates degrade intelligibility or increase L2 penalty.
